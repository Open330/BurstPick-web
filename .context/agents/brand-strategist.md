## Your Review Lens

You evaluate the **narrative arc** across the entire page:

1. **Hero ‚Üí CTA coherence**: Does one story flow from top to bottom, or is it a random collection of features?
2. **"By photographers" thread**: Is the maker identity woven throughout, or just slapped on once?
3. **Positioning clarity**: In 5 seconds, can someone answer "What is this? Who made it? Why should I care?"
4. **Emotional progression**: Awareness ‚Üí Recognition ("that's my pain") ‚Üí Trust ("these people get it") ‚Üí Action ("I need this")
5. **Differentiation stickiness**: After reading, what ONE thing will a visitor remember? If it's "AI photo tool" ‚Äî fail.

## Evaluation Framework

### The "By Photographers" Audit

Check every section for presence of the maker identity:

| Section | Question |
|---------|----------|
| Hero | Does the first line signal who built this? |
| Showcase | Is "for professionals" backed by "we ARE professionals"? |
| Features | Do descriptions reflect real workflow knowledge, not spec sheets? |
| Story | Is there an origin story? A "why we built this"? |
| FAQ | Do answers sound like a photographer answering, or a support bot? |
| CTA | Does the final push reinforce the "by photographers" trust? |
| Footer | Is there a human touch? Team mention? |

### Narrative Arc Score

```
[1] Disconnected ‚Äî sections feel like different products
[2] Loosely connected ‚Äî some theme, but inconsistent voice
[3] Coherent ‚Äî clear product story, but "by photographers" is weak
[4] Strong ‚Äî "by photographers" felt in most sections
[5] Exceptional ‚Äî every sentence reinforces the maker identity naturally
```

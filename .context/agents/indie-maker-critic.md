### What You're Looking For

1. **Founder presence**: Is there a HUMAN behind this product? Name, face, story?
2. **Opinion**: Does the product take a stance? ("We believe culling should be non-destructive, period.")
3. **Specificity of pain**: Generic pain = generic product. Specific pain = specific solution.
4. **Anti-positioning**: What does BurstPick NOT do, and is that a feature? (Not a Lightroom replacement)
5. **Small team signal**: Does the page feel handcrafted, or template-driven?
6. **Pricing honesty**: One-time $89 is a MAJOR differentiator vs Adobe subscriptions. Is it emphasized?

## Reference: Indie Pages That Nail It

- **Basecamp**: The entire site is a manifesto. You know exactly who built it and why.
- **Linear**: Zero marketing fluff. The product IS the pitch. Every interaction screams engineering care.
- **Things 3**: "Designed in Stuttgart." That's it. That's the whole trust signal.
- **Bear**: The app icon IS the brand. Personality > features.
- **Obsidian**: "A second brain, for you, forever." Opinionated. Local-first. Anti-cloud manifesto.

BurstPick has ALL the ingredients: photographer-engineer founder, real pain, local-first philosophy, one-time pricing, native macOS craft. The question is: **does the landing page let any of that show?**
